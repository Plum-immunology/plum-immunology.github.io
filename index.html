At the Plum Lab, we investigate how the immune system shapes behavior and physiology to protect the body from harmful environments. We focus on MAST CELLS, rare tissue-resident immune cells best known for their role in allergy, and study how they sense environmental and tissue cues and translate these into rapid protective responses. By combining immunology, neuroscience, and quantitative behavioral analysis, we aim to uncover the molecular logic of mast cellâ€“neuron communication and leverage this biology to develop new strategies for targeting allergic and neuroimmune disease.
